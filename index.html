Four plausible arcs

Cooperative Abundance

AI is aligned enough, clean power scales, open standards win. Poverty and disease plunge; biodiversity stabilizes; creativity blooms.

≈ 30%

Patchwork of Islands

Fragmented regions & networks. Lots of local innovation; uneven prosperity; progress without a single global order.

≈ 35%

Managed Descent

Climate overshoot + limits force a planned “power‑lean” civilization. Circularity, shorter supply chains, slower tech cycles.

≈ 20%

Failure Modes

Major wars, unaligned AI, bioevents, or cascading climate shocks. Humanity survives with setbacks; likely reverts to Patchwork.

≈ 15%

Time horizons & probable milestones

2025–2055 · The Refactor

AI becomes general‑purpose (on‑device + edge); robotics spreads in logistics, care, and agriculture.

Grids strain; SMR fission, geothermal, and wind/solar + long‑duration storage expand; district waste‑heat reuse grows.

Local‑first, cloud‑optional software patterns mature; personal servers & community micro‑datacenters appear.

Cities retrofit for heat, water, flood resilience; governance experiments: data trusts, open identity/consent protocols.

2055–2100 · Light‑touch Planetary Engineering

Precision biotech normalizes; pandemics are containable with rapid, open countermeasures.

Materials revolution: recyclable composites, programmable matter; near‑zero‑waste manufacturing.

Off‑world economy shifts to utility in LEO/cislunar; Earth remains cultural and biological home.

Longevity improves; “lifelong guilds” and simulators become core education.

2100–2200 · Civic Renaissance

Carbon‑free energy dominates; atmospheric CO₂ peaks then trends down; ocean/soil restoration at scale.

Network polities interoperate; reputation & reciprocity travel with you.

2200–3025 · New Branches

Some humans augment deeply; others choose baseline paths; culture cycles through new “cathedral projects.”

Off‑world habitats remain niche but enduring; Earth thrives if earlier bets succeeded.

Hinges that decide the arc

AI alignment & governance: steerable, auditable, power‑aware systems—or not.

Energy abundance vs. scarcity: <$20/MWh clean baseload is a civilizational cheat code.

Biosecurity norms: open science with strong guardrails.

Climate action pace: adaptation + drawdown before feedbacks lock in.

Interoperability vs. enclosure: right‑to‑repair, open protocols, portability.

War/peace cycles: institutions that contain crises.

What to build this decade

Community cloud + waste‑heat reuse: open‑hardware micro‑datacenters that heat buildings and run local AI.

Local‑first stacks: browser‑as‑OS patterns, offline‑sync, identity/consent built‑in.

Open devices: RISC‑V kits, repairable laptops/phones/ebikes; full BOMs & service manuals.

Education guilds: apprenticeships teaching kernel, electronics, web, and civic tech via real projects.

Resilience hubs: maker‑coops for repair, water, food‑growing tech, and neighborhood energy.
